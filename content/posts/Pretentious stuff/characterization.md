## A nationwide first data baseline

### About this project

![Characterization](/characterization.png)

This is a first attempt to generate a nationwide data baseline of the mechanical, mineralogical and morphological characterization of Ecuadorian soil. With this aim, the analysis of unaltered soil samples corresponding to the capitals of twenty-three provinces and six other significant locations in Ecuador is performed as follows:

First, the gradation and Atterberg limits allow us to obtain each sample’s classification according to the Unified Soil Classification System (USCS), which provides important information regarding soil’s mechanical behavior. Second, unconsolidated undrained (UU) tri-axial tests are carried out in order to determine soil’s strength, i.e., internal friction angle and cohesion. Third, chemical and mineralogical features are inferred by means of energy dispersive spectroscopy (EDS) in a scanning electron microscope (SEM).

![Characterization](/samples.png)

Fourth, the morphology of the grains that furnish the fabric of each given soil sample is obtained by calculating parameters such as roundness, sphericity, grain volume, and grain surface area. Here, through a 3D X-ray computed tomography (3DXRCT) of the samples, the actual shape of each grain is first retrieved (by turning the 3DXRCT voxel-based images into mathematical functions called Level Sets (LS)), calculations on the morphology of the grains are performed. With the output parameters from the four steps that conform the aforementioned analysis of soil samples, a database is created to generate more accurate accurate and sophisticated computer models with enhanced predictive capabilities and improved micro-structure evaluation of real physical samples.

![Characterization](/jamGrains.png)

These new models could eventually replace both: the need for extensive, costly laboratory tests to understand real natural phenomena; and the use of phenomenological models that fail to incorporate micro-mechanical and morphological effects, that are key when it comes to the analysis of the materials behavior. The creation of this dataset is the first step within an overall effort where much remains to be done.

A scientific paper came out of this work. It is currently under review on the Acta Geotecnica journal and we are waiting for a response.
